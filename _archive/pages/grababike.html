Grab a Bike

2010

Design, PHP, HTML, CSS

Grab a Bike was an ambitious school project to research and develop a sustainable bike sharing program for the University of Illinois campus, much like Chicago's Divvy Bikes and New York's Citi Bikes. It spanned three semesters and had some motivated students behind the project to develop prototypes and reach out to the student body as well as the campus transportation service. Unfortunately, like many college projects, everyone graduates and moves on to other things.

I took on the task to design and develop the website for the project. The goal was to create a clean and inviting website that didn't overwhelm the visitor and provided just the right amount of information to get our point across. Being a student of Computer Science, I was running Linux at the time, so my design tools of choice were of course GIMP and Inkscape :). I would later find out that my little Eee PC did not render true colors, so the high contrast of the site is due to trying to reach the right colors on my muted 10" screen!

The only real technical aspect of the site was writing a quick Twitter RSS feed parser in PHP, which is now defunct due to the new Twitter API. All in all, a quick and dirty job!

30 Minute Baseline Zebra Bars™

This is the base recipe to make real, natural energy bricks for hours of enjoyment. You should be able to get from preparation to wrapped bars in about 30 minutes or less. Very procrastination-friendly. Spend less, eat more, rip ass.

Nutrition facts:

  • Serving size: 1 beast
  • Calories: probably a lot
  • Carbohydrates: a heckin' lot
  • Fat: yes you are and you should feel bad
  • Protein: you'll appreciate this on a long ride
  • Delicious: yes
  • Cheap: and how

You will need:

  • Sticky icky glutinous rice. That Chinatown stuff.
  • Chia seeds. Never forget.
  • Chocolate chips. Or cacao nibs if you feeling fancy.
  • Peanut butter.
  • Honey.
  • Coconut flakes. Always tropical.
  • Parchment paper.
  • Aluminum foil.

Make it:

  • Combine rice and chia seeds. How much to use? I don't know. Within reason?
  • Cook the rice the same way you would cook regular rice: 2:1 water:rice ratio, simmer for 15 minutes or so, fluff with a fork. You would.
  • Meanwhile, cut parchment paper and aluminum foil into something like 5"x7" sheets and layer each parchment paper sheet over each foil sheet. You'll get the hang of how large these sheets really should be.
  • Slap rice onto a large cutting board, slather with honey and mix in chocolate chips.
  • Form the magic energy dough into a large rectangular shape, about 1 inch thick. I usually use a butter knife to get the sides nice and straight.
  • Use said butter knife (ah ha!) to spread a nice layer of peanut butter evenly on top of your energy cake.
  • Sprinkle coconut flakes evenly over the peanut butter to cover it and keep it from getting everywhere.
  • Use the knife again or a spatula to cut the muscle cake into approximately 2.5"x4" rectangles.
  • Wrap each brick in the parchment/foil combo in the style of Skratch Labs portables.
  • Behold the heft of your fresh new Zebra Bar.
  • Go shred.

Notes:

These bars can be refrigerated and saved for later. In fact, the rice hardens a bit and becomes easier to handle on the bike.

Experimenting with the recipe and adding or removing ingredients is highly encouraged for variety. I sometimes sprinkle nuts over the peanut butter. I've even made fried rice before. It was okay.
